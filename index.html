If you didn't know

Game of Thrones is an adaptation of the series of fantasy novels known as A Song of Ice and Fire, written by George R. R. Martin. Proceed.

Where at?

The world of Game of Thrones consists of 4 continents, as seen on this map. However, majority of the show takes place on Westeros (left region). On this magical land is a realm known as the Seven Kingdoms, which covers most of the continent. The remaining area is the northernmost part, referred to as Beyond The Wall (white region).

Westeros

As mentioned above, Westeros is made up of 2 regions: the Seven Kingdoms and Beyond The Wall. We'll have a brief look at each.

Seven Kingdoms

The Seven Kingdoms, despite its name, is actually made up of nine regions: The North, The Iron Islands, The Riverlands, The Vale, The Westerlands, The Crownlands, The Reach, The Stormlands, and Dorne. Each of these regions contain many houses (families), but only one of the houses in each region hold authority over their respective area - they are known as the Great Houses. So, with nine regions come nine Great Houses. We'll go over those in a bit.

Beyond The Wall

An extremely large wall separates The Seven Kingdoms from the area to the north and is defended by a military order known as the Night's Watch. The people living north of the wall are called the Free Folk, and are referred to as wildlings. These guys aren't fans of the people in the south or their feudal system and choose to follow only those they want to. Those in the Seven Kingdoms share a similar opinion about their "free" culture beyond the wall.

Who is in charge?

The Seven Kingdoms social structure begins with the King and and moves down all the way to the commoners.

The King

The ruler of the Seven Kingdoms sits on the Iron Throne and holds political authority and final say in all decisions. The Iron Throne is located in King's Landing, the capital of the Seven Kingdoms. The house of whomever is the King is called the Royal House. The battle for the Iron Throne is central to the show as everyone wants that power and influence over the Seven Kingdoms.

Hand of the King

The Hand is appointed by and the advisor to the King and holds the second strongest position in the Seven Kingdoms. If the King is absent, The Hand can make decisions on his/her behalf.

Small Council

The Small Council is a group appointed by and advisor to the King and the King's Hand. When decisions need to be made regarding the economy or law, the King and Hand usually get together with the Small Council to consider their options.

Great Houses

As mentioned above, each of the nine regions in the Seven Kingdoms have a Great House that have authority over their respective region. The head of each house can be considered the ruler over that region and they make decisions on local matters.

Vassals

Each of the Great Houses have vassals, which are just smaller houses that have pledged an allegiance to that Great House. These vassals in turn could have their own vassals, and so on. The rulers of the lower-level vassals report to the head of the vassal above them, and the heads of the top layer of vassals report to the head of their Great House.

Commoners

The commoners, also known as the smallfolk, are at the bottom of the social structure and own no land or titles. They obey their local lord and have no say in any decisions whatsoever.

The Houses

While there are hundreds of houses in the Seven Kingdoms, we will be looking at the select few that are the focus of the show. Hovering over each shield/sigil gives you a brief overview of the house, but if you want to get to know the family members, go ahead and click a shield.



House Baratheon

They are the Great House of The Stormlands, and the head of the house is Robert Baratheon - the King. As the Royal House, they become the Great House of The Crownlands by default. The Royal House also holds three seats (castles): Storm's end in The Stormlands, Red Keep in King's Landing, and Dragonstone in Blackwater Bay.
House Arryn

They are the Great House of The Vale, and the head of the house is Jon Arryn, who is also the Hand of the King. As mentioned earlier, that means he holds the 2nd most powerful position in the Seven Kingdoms. Their seat (castle) is The Eyrie in The Vale.
House Martell

They are the Great House of Dorne, and their leader is Doran Martell. Their seat is Sunspear in Dorne.
House Stark

They are the Great House of The North, and their leader is Eddard Stark. They are one of the oldest houses in the Seven Kingdoms. Their seat is Winterfell in The North.
House Bolton

They are one of the vassals of House Stark, and the head of the house is Roose Bolton. They are known for flaying their enemies (removing layers of their skin). See: their sigil. Their seat is The Dreadfort in The North.
House Greyjoy

They are the Great House of The Iron Islands, and the head of the house is Balon Greyjoy. Their seat is Pyke on Pyke Island (one of The Iron Islands).


House Lannister

They are the Great House of The Westerlands, and the head of the house is Tywin Lannister. They are one of the oldest and richest houses in the Seven Kingdoms. Their seat is Casterly Rock in The Westerlands.
House Tyrell

They are the Great House of The Reach, and their leader is Mace Tyrell. Their seat is Highgarden in The Reach.
House Tully

They are the Great House of The Riverlands, and their leader is Edmure Tully. Their seat is Riverrun in The Riverlands.
House Frey

They are one of the vassals of House Tully, and the head of the house is Walder Frey. Their seat is The Twins in The Riverlands.
House Targaryen

They were the former Royal House of the Seven Kingdoms (and thus the Great House of The Crownlands) for nearly 300 years before the events of the show. Their leader is Daenerys Targaryen.

Robert's Rebellion

Remember how I mentioned at the beginning this show has a large family tree, thus making it difficult to understand at first? Well, the top of the family tree (or at least from where it matters to us) requires an understanding of the events that took place before the series. Robert's Rebellion is a war that happened some time before the show. Full details are available here, but I will attempt to summarize the article.

House Taragaryen was a dynasty that ruled the Seven Kingdoms for 300 years. Aerys Targaryen was the King right before Robert's Rebellion. He had 2 kids with Rhaella Targaryen: Rhaegar and Viserys. He was on the crazy side, having people killed left and right, and houses rebelled against him. His son Rhaegar was married to Elia Martell (Targaryen-Martell alliance), but said fuck her, and went and abducted Lyanna Stark, who was engaged to Robert Baratheon (Stark-Baratheon alliance).

As expected, the Starks and Baratheons didn't appreciate that very much, and wanted answers. Lyanna's brother (Brandon Stark) was arrested for treason, and Aerys requested Lyanna's father (Rickard Stark) show up to court. When he got there, both Brandon and Rickard were murdered.

House Arryn, Baratheon, and Stark, who all rebelled against Aerys, formed an alliance with House Tully through marriage. Lyanna's other brother, Eddard Stark, married Caetlyn Tully, and Jon Arryn, the head of House Arryn, married Lysa Tully. Robert Baratheon ended up killing Rhaegar during a large fight known as the Battle of the Trident.

Elia Martell, meanwhile, was being kept by Aerys to make sure the Martells didn't ditch House Targaryen during the war. During the Sack of King's Landing, where House Lannister betrayed House Targaryen and sacked the city, Jamie Lannister killed Aerys, thus becoming known as the King Slayer. Elia was raped and murdered by Gregor Clegane,also known as The Mountain. Lyanna also died during the war, but the reasons are unknown.

Robert claimed the Iron Throne and married Cersei Lannister as a thanks to House Lannister for taking down the Targaryens, and Jon Arryn was named the Hand of the King. Rhaella Targaryen, wife of Aerys, fled to Dragonstone with her son Viserys while pregnant, where she gave birth to her and (now late) Aerys's third child, Daenerys Targaryen. Rhaella died shortly after. That brings us to season 1, and I can now move on to the family tree.

Tywin Lannister

The head of House Lannister and has 3 children with Joanna Lannister: Cersei, Jamie, and Tyrion Lannister. Initially the Hand of the King to Aerys Targaryen, he betrays the Targaryens and forms an alliance with House Baratheon. We'll get into that famous event later.

Cersei Lannister

Cerseri is the Queen of the Seven Kingdoms after marrying Robert Baratheon, with whom she has 3 children: Joffrey, Tommen, and Myrcella Baratheon. She is also the twin sister of Jamie.

Jamie Lannister

Jamie is the eldest son and responsible for killing (then ruler of the Seven Kingdoms) Aerys Targaryen, where he got the name "the Kingslayer". He is the twin brother of Cersei.

Tyrion Lannister

Tyrion is the younger son and was born a dwarf. His mother Joanna died giving birth to him, and thus he has a rocky relationship with most of his family. He enjoys prostitutes and wine.

Other Notables

- Tywin has a younger brother named Kevan, who is also one of his advisors.
- Kevan has a son named Lancel (and two others we don't care about).

Olenna Tyrell

Known as the Queen of Thorns, and the widow of Luthor Tyrell, who died before the events of the show. With Luthor, she has a son named Mace Tyrell. You'll notice that she is very witty and has much influence over matters despite not being the head of the house.

Mace Tyrell

The son of Olenna and Luthor Tyrell, and the head of House Tyrell. With his wife Alerie (not seen in the show), he has two children: Margaery and Loras Tyrell. Not really seen as a leader per say, which is where his mother and children come in.

Margaery Tyrell

Margaery is the only daughter of Mace and Alerie Tyrell. She is both gorgeous and very intelligent.

Loras Tyrell

Loras is the son of Mace and Alerie Tyrell. He is also considered one of the best knights in Westeros.
