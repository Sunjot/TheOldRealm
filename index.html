Welcome.

If you're new to Game of Thrones or a couple episodes in and lost, read on. This show is considered one of the more difficult shows to follow when you're starting out due to the large family trees and the long history.

So, I have created this site to prepare those who wish to begin the series or are having trouble following along. I'll be covering all the important pieces of information that you'll need to know before diving into season 1. Don't worry, it's spoiler-free.

If you didn't know already...

Game of Thrones is an adaptation of the series of fantasy novels known as A Song of Ice and Fire, written by George R. R. Martin. Easy - let's move on.

Where at?

The world of Game of Thrones consists of 4 continents. However, majority of the show takes place on the one known as Westeros (left region on the map). On this magical land is a realm known as the Seven Kingdoms, which covers most of Westeros. The remaining area is the northernmost part of the continent, referred to as Beyond The Wall (white area you see on the map, if you couldn't tell already).



Westeros

As mentioned above, Westeros is made up of 2 regions: the Seven Kingdoms and Beyond The Wall. We'll have a brief look at each.

Seven Kingdoms

The Seven Kingdoms, despite its name, is actually made up of nine regions: The North, The Iron Islands, The Riverlands, The Vale, The Westerlands, The Crownlands, The Reach, The Stormlands, and Dorne. Each of these regions contain many houses (families), but only one of the houses in each region hold authority over their respective area - they are known as the Great Houses. So, with nine regions come nine Great Houses. We'll go over those in a bit.

Beyond The Wall

An extremely large wall separates The Seven Kingdoms from the area to the north and is defended by a military order known as the Night's Watch. The people living north of the wall are called the Free Folk, and are referred to as wildlings. These guys aren't fans of the people in the south or their feudal system and choose to follow only those they want to. Those in the Seven Kingdoms share a similar opinion about their "free" culture beyond the wall.

Who is in charge?

The Seven Kingdoms social structure begins with the King and and moves down all the way to the commoners.

The King

The ruler of the Seven Kingdoms sits on the Iron Throne and holds political authority and final say in all decisions. The Iron Throne is located in King's Landing, the capital of the Seven Kingdoms. The house of whomever is the King is called the Royal House. The battle for the Iron Throne is central to the show as everyone wants that power and influence over the Seven Kingdoms.

Small Council

The Small Council is a group appointed by and advisor to the King and the King's Hand. When decisions need to be made regarding the economy or law, the King and Hand usually get together with the Small Council to consider their options. However, final say is still up to the King - they are only there to provide pros/cons, etc.

Vassals

Each of the Great Houses have multiple vassals, which are just smaller houses that have pledged an allegiance to that Great House. These vassals in turn could have their own vassals, and so on. The rulers of the lower-level vassals (known as landed knights) report to the head of the vassal above them, and the heads of the top layer of vassals report to the head of the Great House they hold fealty to. You get the idea.

Hand of the King

The Hand is appointed by and the advisor to the King and holds the second strongest position in the Seven Kingdoms. If the King is absent, The Hand can make decisions on his/her behalf and even sit on the Iron Throne.

Great Houses

As mentioned above, each of the nine regions in the Seven Kingdoms have a Great House that have authority over their respective region. The head of each house can be considered the "King" over that region. The heads of the families hold the next strongest position in the Seven Kingdoms and make decisions on local matters.

Commoners

The commoners, also known as the smallfolk, are at the bottom of the social structure and own no land or titles. They obey their local lord and have no say in any decisions whatsoever.

The Houses

While there are hundreds of houses in the Seven Kingdoms, there are a select few that are the focus of the show, and we will be looking at those only. You can hover over (or click) each shield/sigil to get a brief overview of the houses.



House Baratheon

They are the Great House of The Stormlands, and the head of the house is Robert Baratheon - the King. Thus, House Baratheon is the Royal House, and therefore also the Great House of The Crownlands by default. The Royal House holds three seats (castles): Storm's end in The Stormlands, Red Keep in King's Landing, and Dragonstone in Blackwater Bay.
House Arryn

They are the Great House of The Vale, and the head of the house is Jon Arryn, who is also the Hand of the King. In that role, he advises the King in decisions made and holds the 2nd most powerful position. Their seat is The Eyrie in The Vale.
House Bolton

They are one of the houses in The North, and the head of the house is Roose Bolton. They are known for flaying their enemies (removing layers of their skin) and letting them rot to their death. See: their sigil. Their seat is The Dreadfort in The North.
House Frey

They are one of the houses in The Riverlands, and the head of the house is Walder Frey. Their seat is The Twins in The Riverlands.
House Greyjoy

They are the Great House of The Iron Islands, and the head of the house is Balon Greyjoy. Their seat is Pyke in Pyke Island (one of The Iron Islands).
House Lannister

They are the Great House of The Westerlands, and the head of the house is Tywin Lannister. They are one of the oldest and richest houses in the Seven Kingdoms. Their seat is Casterly Rock in The Westerlands.


House Martell

They are the Great House of Dorne, and their leader is Doran Martell. Sunspear is a castle in Dorne and the seat of House Martell.
House Tyrell

They are the Great House of The Reach, and their leader is Mace Tyrell. Highgarden is a castle in The Reach and the seat of House Tyrell.
House Stark

They are the Great House of The North, and their leader is Eddard Stark. They are one of the oldest houses in the Seven Kingdoms. Winterfell is a castle in The North and the seat of House Stark.
House Targaryen

They were the former Royal House of the Seven Kingdoms and thus the Great House of The Crownlands for nearly 300 years before the events of the show. Their leader is Daenerys Targaryen.
House Tully

They are the Great House of The Riverlands, and their leader is Edmure Tully. Riverrun is a castle in The Riverlands and the seat of House Tully.
